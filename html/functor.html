Functor

Last modified: December 11, 2025 at 8:05 PM ET
Created: July 09, 2025 at 6:02 PM ET
Tags: category-theory, definition

Idea. Functors are to categories what homomorphisms are to groups: they send objects to objects and morphisms to morphisms while respecting identities and composition, letting us compare different mathematical contexts.

Definition (Functor). Let $\cC, \cD$ be category. A functor $F \colon \cC \to \cD$ consists of:

  • Object mapping: $A \mapsto F(A)$
  • Morphism mapping: $(f \colon A \to B) \mapsto (F(f) \colon F(A) \to F(B))$

preserving identities and composition: \[ F(\id_A) = \id_{F(A)}, \qquad F(g \circ f) = F(g) \circ F(f) \]

Examples

Example (Forgetful functor). $U \colon \Grp \to \Set$ sends each group to its underlying set.

Example (Free functor). $F \colon \Set \to \Grp$ sends $X$ to the free group on $X$.

Example (Hom functor). For any $A \in \cC$: $\Hom(A, -) \colon \cC \to \Set$ is covariant.
