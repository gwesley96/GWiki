Functor

Topics: category-theory, definition
Last modified: 2025-12-09

\begin{idea}[Idea] Functors are to categories what homomorphisms are to groups: they send objects to objects and morphisms to morphisms while respecting identities and composition, letting us compare different mathematical contexts. \end{idea}

Definition (Functor).

Let $\cC, \cD$ be category. A \textbf{functor} $F : \cC \to \cD$ consists of: \begin{lst} \item Object mapping: $A \mapsto F(A)$ \item Morphism mapping: $(f : A \to B) \mapsto (F(f) : F(A) \to F(B))$ \end{lst} preserving identities and composition: \[ F(\id_A) = \id_{F(A)}, \qquad F(g \circ f) = F(g) \circ F(f) \]

\section{Examples}

Example (Forgetful functor).

$U : \Grp \to \Set$ sends each group to its underlying set.

Example (Free functor).

$F : \Set \to \Grp$ sends $X$ to the free group on $X$.

Example (Hom functor).

For any $A \in \cC$: $\Hom(A, -) : \cC \to \Set$ is covariant.
