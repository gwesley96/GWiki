Algebraic Bianchi Identity

Last modified: 2025-12-09

\begin{framedidea}[Core idea] Viewing curvature as a $\mathfrak{gl}$-valued $2$-form forces a Jacobi-type symmetry: cycling the first three inputs of the Riemann tensor gives zero. \end{framedidea}

The \textbf{algebraic Bianchi identity} (AKA \textbf{first Bianchi identity}) is the assertion that the first three slots of the riemann-(04)-curvature-tensor satisfy the jacobi-identity, i.e., that$$\mathrm{Rm}(u,v,w,q)+\mathrm{Rm}(w,u,v,q)+\mathrm{Rm}(v,w,u,q)=0\qquad\forall v,w,u\in\mathfrak{X}(M),$$or in local coordinates, $R_{ijkl}+R_{jkil}+R_{kijl}=0$ for all $i,j,k$.

This is because we can view $R(-,-)$ as a section of $\Omega^{2}(M)\otimes \mathrm{End}(E)$ with coefficients in the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(\mathrm{O}(E_{x}))$; see ((Calegari, p. 53)).
