Algebraic Bianchi Identity

Last modified: 2025-12-09 22:39
Created: 2025-12-09

Idea (Core idea). Viewing curvature as a $\mathfrak{gl}$-valued $2$-form forces a Jacobi-type symmetry: cycling the first three inputs of the Riemann tensor gives zero.

The algebraic Bianchi identity (AKA first Bianchi identity) is the assertion that the first three slots of the Riemann (0,4)-Curvature Tensor satisfy the Jacobi Identity, i.e., that\[\mathrm{Rm}(u,v,w,q)+\mathrm{Rm}(w,u,v,q)+\mathrm{Rm}(v,w,u,q)=0\qquad\forall v,w,u\in\mathfrak{X}(M),\]or in local coordinates, $R_{ijkl}+R_{jkil}+R_{kijl}=0$ for all $i,j,k$.

This is because we can view $R(-,-)$ as a section of $\Omega^{2}(M)\otimes \mathrm{End}(E)$ with coefficients in the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(\mathrm{O}(E_{x}))$; see [1].
