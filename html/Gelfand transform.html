Gelfand transform

Last modified: December 11, 2025 at 8:05 PM ET
Created: 2025-12-09
Tags: functional-analysis, operator-algebras

Idea. Characters on a commutative Banach algebra assemble into a compact space $\widehat{A}$, and evaluating elements on characters turns algebra elements into continuous functions, converting algebraic information into spectral-analytic data.

For a commutative Banach algebra $A$, the Gelfand transform, given by \[ \begin{aligned} A&\to C(\widehat{A}),\\ a&\mapsto[\operatorname{ev}_a\colon \varphi\mapsto\varphi(a)], \end{aligned} \] is a norm-decreasing unital algebra homomorphism, where $\widehat{A}$ is the set of characters (AKA multiplicative linear functionals) on $A$, that is, of nonzero algebra homomorphisms $A\to\mathbb{C}$.

The image of the Gelfand transform is a subalgebra of $C(\widehat{A})$ that separates points of $\widehat{A}$.

If $A$ is a commutative unital Banach algebra and $a\in A$, then for all $\varphi\in\widehat{A}$, $\varphi(a)\in\operatorname{sp}(a)$.
