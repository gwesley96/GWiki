components in presemisimple linear 2-categories

Last modified: December 13, 2025 at 3:59 PM ET
Created: July 01, 2025 at 8:01 PM ET

Components of presemisimple 2-categories. Unlike in the 1-categorical case (see Schur’s Lemma), two simple objects in a presemisimple 2-category $\mathcal{C}$ may be connected by nonzero 1-morphisms.

Two simple objects $a$ and $b$ of $\mathfrak{C}$ are in the same connected component (often just component) of $\mathfrak{C}$ if there is a nonzero 1-morphism $a\to b$, that is if $\mathfrak{C}(a\to b)\neq 0$. The set of components of $\mathfrak{C}$, denoted $\pi_0\mathfrak{C}$, is the quotient of the set of simples by the equivalence relation of being in the same component.

Connectedness is an equivalence relation in a presemisimple 2-category $\mathfrak{C}$: reflexivity is clear, symmetry follows from the fact that the right adjoint of a nonzero morphism is nonzero, and transitivity is precisely the content of Proposition 1.2.19.

Thus each ‘indecomposable’ collection of simples will be completely connected in the sense that there is a nonzero morphism between any two simples in the collection.

Examples

  • (Very important!): For an indecomposable multifusion category $\mathcal{D}$, the associated unfolded 2-category is connected, i.e., has a single component. More generally, the set of indecomposable summands of a multifusion category corresponds to the set of connected components of its unfolding. (In fact, we can view giving a multifusion category as a method for providing the data of a finite presemisimple 2-category.)
