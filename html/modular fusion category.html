modular fusion category

Last modified: December 13, 2025 at 3:59 PM ET
Created: June 07, 2025 at 10:10 PM ET

Idea. Although it is not precise, it is still helpful to think of a modular category as a categorification of a Frobenius algebra.

A modular fusion category (MFC) (AKA modular tensor category (MTC)) is a ribbon fusion category satisfying any (hence all) of the following equivalent conditions.

  • $\mathcal{C}$ is nondegenerate, i.e., the S matrix of $\mathcal{C}$ is invertible.
  • The symmetric center $\mathcal{C}'$ of $\mathcal{C}$ is trivial ($\mathcal{C}'\cong\mathsf{Vec}$).
  • $\mathcal{C}$ is factorizable, i.e., the braided monoidal functor $\mathcal{C}\boxtimes \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{rev}}\to\mathcal{Z}(\mathcal{C})$ given by the Deligne product of the functors $c\mapsto (c,\beta_{c,-})$ and $c\mapsto (c,\beta_{-,c})$ is an equivalence (where $\mathrm{rev}$ denotes $\mathcal{C}$ with braiding $\beta_{x,y}^{\mathrm{rev}}\coloneqq \beta_{y,x}^{^{-1}}$).

Important Remark. A UMTC is fundamentally a category whose objects can be interpreted as collections of anyons and morphisms as anyon processes, together with three coherent structures with precisely the right properties to enforce the axioms of a quantum system: 1) the monoidal structure to define quantum states with properties that allow these states to be superposed and entangled, the pivotal structure to define measurement; 2) the structure of a braiding to describe evolution of states; and 3) unitarity to ensure unitary time evolution.
