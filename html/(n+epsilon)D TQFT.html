$(n+\varepsilon)$D TQFT

Last modified: December 12, 2025 at 6:54 PM ET
Created: August 17, 2025 at 10:09 PM ET
Tags: disk-like framework, TQFT, skein-modules, higher-categories

Definition ($(n+\varepsilon)$-dimensional TQFT). An $(n+\varepsilon)$-dimensional TQFT $(\F,\U)$ is just an $n$-dimensional system of topological fields and local relations.

The reason for this terminology is that although an $(n+\varepsilon)$-dimensional TQFT does not have the data of the path integral/partition function of a typical $(n+1)$-dimensional manifold, it does have this data for mapping cylinders between $n$-manifolds.

Skein modules and skein $k$-categories

Construction (Skein module from an $(n+\varepsilon)$-dimensional TQFT). Let $(\F,\U)$ be an $(n+\varepsilon)$D TQFT and consider an $n$-manifold $X$ with boundary condition $c\in\F(\partial X)$. Let $R$ be any unital ring. We define the skein module associated to $(X,c)$ by the free $R$-module generated by fields on $X$ (in this context, often called base fields) modulo local relations on $(X,c)$, i.e., \[ \A_{\F,\U}(X,c;R)\coloneqq R[\F(X,c)]/\U(X,c). \] By a slight abuse of terminology, we will often refer to elements of $\A_{\F,\U}(X,c;R)$ again as fields.

Construction (Skein $k$-category from an $(n+\e)$D TQFT). More generally, for any $1 \leq k \leq n$ we can consider $(W,c)$ for any $(n - k)$-manifold $W$ and any boundary condition $d\in\F(\partial Y)$. We define the skein $k$-category $\A_{\F,\U}(W;d;R)$ to be the following (traditional, algebraic, linear) $R$-linear $k$-category whose morphisms we define recursively as follows.

  • 0-morphisms. The objects of $\A_{\F,\U}(W,d;R)$ are elements of the subset $\F(W;d)\coloneqq \partial^{-1}(d)$ of $\F(W)$.
  • $j$-morphisms for $1\leq j\leq n-1$. For any two compatible $(j-1)$-morphisms $a,b$ in $\A_{\F,\U}(W,d;R)$, we set $\A_{\F,\U}(W,d;R)(a\to b)\coloneqq \F(W\times D^j,(\fcj a, b);R)$.
  • $n$-morphisms. For any two compatible $(n-1)$-morphisms $a,b$ in $\A_{\F,\U}(W,d;R)$, we set $\A_{\F,\U}(W,d;R)(a\to b)\coloneqq \A_{\F,\U}(W\times D^j,(\fcj a, b);R)$.

As usual, in this construction, unless $\partial W=\varnothing$, every product $W\times D^j$ has a “pinched” boundary so that $\partial (W\times D^{j})\cong \Delta W\coloneqq \overline{W}\cup_{\partial W} W$, the double of $Y$. (Note that we have not showed how $j$-composition works in general, but composition is given by gluing.)

For the rest of this article we take $R$ to be the complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$, so we will henceforth drop $R$ from the notation.

We will be more interested in the dual space (representations) of $\A_{\F,\U}(X,c)$, which we denote by $Z_{\F,\U}(X,c)$. This is because $Z_{\F,\U}(X,c)$ turn out to have nicer algebraic structure than the $\A_{\F,\U}(X,c)$-invariants; in particular, at least for $n=1,2$, the former are always Cauchy complete even when the latter are not.

Notation. Often the system of fields and local relations $(\F,\U)$ that define $\A_{\F,\U}$ and $Z_{\F,\U}$ are should be clear from context, and we simply write $\A$ and $Z$ respectively.

Examples

Our main example of an $n$-dimensional system of fields will be $\cC$-labeled string diagrams for some “traditional $n$-category with strong duality” $\cC$.
