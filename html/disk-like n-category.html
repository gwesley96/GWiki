disk-like n-category

Last modified: December 12, 2025 at 7:42 PM ET
Created: September 29, 2025 at 6:53 PM ET

Let $H$ be a given geometric structure (e.g., none, orientation, spin, pin, $\dots$). All manifolds are assumed to be PL. All $k$-manifolds are assumed equipped with the germ of a thickening to an $n$-manifold and a choice of $H$-structure and an $n$-framing in the thickening. By “homeomorphism” we mean $H$-structure-preserving PL homeomorphism of the (germ of the thickened) manifold. By a “$k$-ball” or “$k$-disk” we mean a $k$-manifold that is homeomorphic to the closed $k$-ball $D^n$ in $\bbR^{k}$ via some (unspecified!) homeomorphism. Denote by $\overline{\,\cdot\,}$ the $H$-structure-reversal involutive endofunctor on the groupoid $\{k\text{-balls and homeomorphisms}\}$.

Definition. An (unenriched, $H$-pivotal) disk-like $n$-category $\cC$ consists of the following data.

  • For each $0\leq k \leq n$, a functor $\cC^{k}\colon \{k\text{-balls and homeomorphisms}\}\to \mathsf{Set}$.
  • For each $k\leq n$, a conjugation natural isomorphism $\widehat{\;\cdot\;}\colon \cC^{k}(\overline{\,\cdot\,})\cong \cC^{k}$.
  • For each $(k-1)$-ball $Y$ in the boundary of a $k$-ball $X$, a restriction map $\cC^{k}(X)\to \cC^{k-1}(Y)$ (defined on sufficiently large subsets of $\cC^{k}$).
  • For each $k$-ball $X$ obtained by gluing two $k$-balls $X_{1}$ and $X_{2}$ along a common $(k-1)$-ball $Y$ in their boundaries, a composition/gluing map $\cC^{k}(X_{1})\times_{\cC^{k-1}(Y)}\cC^{k}(X_2)\to \cC^{k}(X)$.
  • For each $k$-ball $X$ and $m$-ball $D$ (with $k+m\leq n$), a product map $\cC^{k}(X)\to \cC^{k+m}(X\times D)$ taking a morphism $a$ to the product/identity morphism $a\times D$.

These data are subject to the following conditions.

  • The gluing maps are strictly associative and are compatible (commute) with the actions of diffeomorphisms.
  • The product maps are associative and are compatible with gluing, restrictions, and diffeomorphisms.
  • Diffeomorphisms of $n$-balls that are isotopic rel boundary act identically; the same holds for collar maps.
  • Morphisms are splittable: for any $k$-morphism $c$ (with $k<n$), there exists an embedded cell complex $S_c$ (the string locus) within its underlying ball, such that $c$ is splittable along any decomposition that is transverse to $S_c$.

Example ($\cC$-labeled string diagrams). Our main example of an $n$-dimensional system of fields will be $\cC$-labeled string diagrams for some “traditional $n$-category with strong duality” $\cC$. Recall that a $\cC$-labeled string diagram on a $k$-manifold $Y$ is a general-position embedded cell complex of $Y$ whose codimension-$j$ cells are labeled by $j$-morphisms of $\cC$.
