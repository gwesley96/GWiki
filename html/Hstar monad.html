Definition.

An \defn{$\mathrm{H}^*$-monad} in a pre-3-Hilbert space $(\mathfrak{X},\vee,\Psi)$ consists of an object $a\in\fX$ and a 1-morphism ${}_aA_a\in \Omega_a$ equipped with an associative multiplication $\mu\colon {}_aA\otimes_aA_a\to {}_aA_a$ with unit $\iota\colon 1_a\to {}_aA_a$ satisfying the following axioms. \begin{lst}[label=(H$^*$\arabic*)] \item ($\rC^*$-Frobenius) $\mu^\dag$ is an $A$--$A$-bimodule map, i.e., \[ \begin{tkz}[scale=0.3] \coordinate (O) at (0,0); Definition.

A \defn{splitting} of an $\rH^*$-monad $({}_aA_a,\mu,\iota)$ in $\fX$ is a pair $({}_aX_b,\gamma)$ consisting of a 1-morphism ${}_aX_b\in\fX(a\to b)$ such that $\ev_X\ev_X^\dag \in \End_\fX(1_b)$ is invertible and a unitary monad isomorphism $\gamma\colon {}_aA_a\Rightarrow {}_aX\otimes_b X^\vee_a$.
