Fix a monoidal category $(\mathcal{V},\otimes_{\mathcal{V}},1_{\mathcal{V}})$. (It is helpful to think of $(\mathrm{Vec},\otimes_{\mathbb{C}},\mathbb{C})$.)
A $\mathcal{V}$-\textbf{enriched category} consists of the following data.
- A collection of \textbf{0-cells} $a\in\mathcal{C}$.
- For each pair of 0-cells $a,b\in\mathcal{C}$, a \textbf{hom} 0-cell $\mathcal{C}(a\to b)\in\mathcal{V}$; its \textbf{elements} or \textbf{1-cells} $f\in\mathcal{C}(a\to b)$ are 1-cells $f \in \mathcal{V}(1_{\mathcal{V}}\to \mathcal{C}(a\to b))$.
- For each triplet of 0-cells $a,b,c\in\mathcal{C}$, a \textbf{1-composition} 1-cell$$\circ\in\mathcal{V}(\mathcal{C}(b\to c)\otimes_{\mathcal{V}}\mathcal{C}(a\to b)\to \mathcal{C}(a\to b)).$$
- For each 0-cell $a\in\mathcal{C}$, an \textbf{identity} element $j_a\in\mathcal{V}(1_{\mathcal{V}}\to \mathcal{C}(a\to a))$.

These data are subject to the following conditions.
- The $\circ$ are strictly associative.
- The $\circ$ strictly preserve identity 1-cells in the left and right slots.
