\paragraph*{In $(\infty,1)$-categories}
Let $C$ be an \wref{(∞,1)-category} with a \wref[terminal object]{terminal object in an (∞,1)-category} $*$.

For any \wref{pointed object} $X$ in $C$, its \textbf{loop space object} (AKA \textbf{looping}) of $X$, denoted $\Omega X$, is defined as the \wref[(∞,1)-pullback]{(∞,1)-pullback in an (∞,1)-category} of the point $x_{0}$ along itself in $C$:
\begin{tikzcd}
\Omega X \arrow[r] \arrow[d] & * \arrow[d, "x_0"] \\
* \arrow[r, "x_0"'] & X
\end{tikzcd}
A \textbf{delooping} of an object $A\in C$ is a pointed object $\mathrm{B}A$ such that there is an \wref[equivalence]{equivalence in an (∞,n)-category} $A \simeq \Omega(\mathrm{B}A)$ in $C$.
