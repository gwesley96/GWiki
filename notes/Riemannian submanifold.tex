For any \wref[isometric embedding]{isometric immersion} $i\colon N^k\to M^n$, we call $N$ a \defn{$k$-dimensional Riemannian submanifold} and identify $N$ with its embedded image $i(N)\subset M$. In this case we call $M$ the \defn{ambient space} and say that the Riemannian submanifold $N$ has \defn{codimension} $n-k$ in the ambient space $M$. 

\begin{example}
The $n$-sphere $S^n$ is a codimension-$1$ Riemannian submanifold of $\bbR^{n+1}$.
\end{example}
