\Title{smooth manifold}
\Tags{}

\begin{document}
\NoteHeader

An atlas is called a \textbf{smooth atlas} if \textit{all} transition maps are smooth. An $m$-dimensional \wref{topological manifold} $M$ is \textbf{smooth} if $M$ admits a \wref{smooth atlas}.

A topological manifold with boundary is \textbf{smooth} if there exists an atlas $\{(U_\alpha,\phi_\alpha)\}$ such that all transition functions$$\phi_\alpha\circ\phi_\beta^{-1}:\mathbb{R}_+^n\to\mathbb{R}_+^n$$are smooth, which is to say there exists a smooth extension of $\phi_\alpha\circ\phi_\beta^{-1}$ to an open neighborhood of $\mathbb{R}^n_+$ in $\mathbb{R}^n.$
