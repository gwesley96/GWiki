\textbf{Example.} In a \wref{presemisimple 2-category}, any two objects $a$ and $b$ in the same \wref[connected component]{components in presemisimple linear 2-categories} condense onto each other: from a 1-morphism $f\colon a\to b$, the existence of adjoints means that there is an $f^{R}\colon b\to a$, and one can show that $(f,f^{R})$ is part of the data of a \wref[condensation]{n-condensation in an n-category} $\,\longrightarrow\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\hookleftarrow{\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\color{white}\blacksquare\color{black}}\,\,b\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!a\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,$.
